About Me

Welcome to my page! I'm Sameen, and I'm a computer science student at Umass Amherst. I am passionate about technology and have a strong interest in exploring its endless possibilities. When I'm not working on a project, you can find me on the tennis court or at the gym.

A Little More About Me

I first got exposed to web development during my freshman year of high school, and it ignited my curiosity and passion for creating websites and applications. In my sophomore year, I participated in the MATLAB STEM challenge where we built a fitness tracker by collecting raw data and using algorithms.

After my senior year, I had the opportunity to intern at a company where I worked with license plates and developed code in Java to distinguish between counterfeit and real identification. To do this, I was tasked with testing hundreds of counterfeit and real IDs through the identification scanner. I then uploaded these scans, including UV pictures to the cloud while encrypting them to avoid unauthorized access.

I have a burning passion for technology and a relentless drive to keep learning and growing within the field. Whether it's exploring backend development or improving my front-end skills, I am always eager to expand my knowledge and create meaningful and impactful projects. For this reason I am continuing to go out of my comfort zone building projects with technologies I have not used before. I may not be perfect, but I'm committed to putting in the effort and dedication required to succeed.
